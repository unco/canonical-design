A cohesive approach to design at Canonical

Managing a design system that is owned by several teams is challenging.

This is why we created a hierarchy of subsystems tha assign ownership to their relevant teams. Our approach to delivering design quality

Defined quality standards

We aim for our design system to set the standard for open source design. To do this, we set clear rules for what good quality means. We then use these rules to check our designs, code, and documentation and make sure that our design system maintains high standards.

Design and code documentation

We check how complete our documentation is to see if it's correct. We share the guidelines with users even if they're not perfect, helping users save time and focus on what matters. This practical approach helps us cover more ground and improve our documentation over time.

Measuring our output

Metrics help us understand how the design system is being used in our team. We check how often our teams use standard parts versus making their own. It helps us see if our standard parts need improvement. We also keep track of how much each team adds to our system. This keeps everyone involved and helps us spread the work across more teams.

Accessibility

We care about making our components follow accessibility rules, to facilitate complying with standards in our products. Read more about how we tackle accessibility at Canonical.

About the design system team

Our Design System team consists of UX designers, UX writers, Engineers and Visual designers, who spend 20% of their time ensuring consistency and quality across our design systems. Our web engineers focus on Vanilla and for Ubuntu and its desktop apps, we team up with Flutter developers and the community.
